## Observations (eventual)

- **Correctness ✓** — all services reported identical filter hits (`bill_text`=1, `bill_amendment`=0) every run.
- **Eventual fixes Turbopuffer's earlier handicap.** On eventual it's the steadiest performer:
  p50 ~6–11ms, tightest tails of all four. (Strong rows pending will quantify the consistency tax.)
- **Pinecone** — good medians but persistent tail spikes (max into the 300–470ms range).
- **Qdrant Cloud** — still shows the ~50ms cold-segment plateau that dominates its avg/p50 at low
  topK; only at topK=50 (more compute, plateau amortized) does it look competitive.
- **OpenSearch** — fast and tight (private in-VPC), but watch the occasional outlier (one 1310ms
  max at topK=5/iters=50) — managed-domain GC/segment-merge noise.

## Strong vs eventual (Turbopuffer only)
- Strong consistency adds a **~10–15ms tax** (~2–3×): Turbopuffer p50 goes from ~6–11ms (eventual)
  to ~17–22ms (strong) — the object-storage consistency round-trip.
- Even taxed, strong Turbopuffer (~17–22ms p50) stays competitive: ≈ Pinecone, faster than Qdrant's
  cold-segment plateau, slower than in-VPC OpenSearch.
- This dataset is static, so eventual returns identical results — **eventual is the right default**
  unless you need read-your-writes. Pinecone/Qdrant/OpenSearch are eventual-only (`—`).
